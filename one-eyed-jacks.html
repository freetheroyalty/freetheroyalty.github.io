One-Eyed Jacks

One-Eyed Jacks is a 1961 American Western film directed by Marlon Brando and produced by Stanley Kubrick. It is based on the novel The Authentic Death of Hendry Jones by Charles Neider. The film follows the story of a former outlaw named Rio (Brando) who returns to his hometown in search of revenge against his former partner, Dad Longworth (Karl Malden), who betrayed him and left him to be captured by the authorities. Rio enlists the help of a group of outlaws and together they plan to rob the bank in town, but as the heist unfolds, Rio is faced with difficult choices that test his loyalty and morality. The film features an ensemble cast including Brando, Malden, Katy Jurado, Ben Johnson, and Slim Pickens. One-Eyed Jacks is considered a classic of the Western genre and is remembered for its compelling performances, beautiful cinematography, and complex themes.
