The Mummy

The Mummy is a 1932 horror film directed by Karl Freund and starring Boris Karloff as the titular character, Imhotep. The film is based on the novel by Nina Wilcox Putnam and Richard Schayer and follows the story of an ancient Egyptian priest who is resurrected and seeks revenge on those who have disturbed his tomb. Imhotep is brought back to life through a series of elaborate ceremonies and embarks on a mission to find the reincarnation of his lost love, Princess Ankh-es-en-amon. Along the way, he confronts a group of archaeologists and a young woman named Helen Grosvenor, who bears a striking resemblance to the princess. The Mummy is a classic of the horror genre and is known for its atmospheric sets, creepy atmosphere, and Karloff's memorable performance as the undead Imhotep.
